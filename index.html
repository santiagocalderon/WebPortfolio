About Me

Greetings, I'm Santiago Calderon, an Electrical Engineering student at the University of Florida slated to graduate in Spring '24. My academic journey spans a diverse landscape, but my expertise converges at the intersection of Power Systems, Electromagnetics, and Machine Learning. In the realm of Power, my proficiency extends from Power Converter and Rectifier Analysis/Design to Motor/Generator Analysis/Design. I navigate the intricacies of circuits and solid-state devices with precision, leveraging a deep understanding of applied magnetics to drive innovative solutions. Electromagnetics forms the core of my skill set, encompassing digital design using VHDL, digital logic, and a comprehensive grasp of electromagnetic principles. This foundation extends seamlessly into applied magnetics and remote sensing, reflecting a holistic understanding of the electromagnetic spectrum. In the dynamic field of Machine Learning, I actively contribute to the GatorSense Machine Learning Laboratory, where my focus lies in algorithm implementation, model validation, and fine-tuning. My journey in control systems adds a nuanced layer to my expertise, allowing me to bridge the gap between theory and practical application. Beyond academia, my leadership role in the Miami Coral Park FIRST Robotics Team underscores my ability to manage and direct teams for successful project execution. Armed with proficiency in modeling software like AutoCAD and SolidWorks and a mastery of PCB design using LTSpice and Altium, I embark on a relentless pursuit of knowledge. My goal is to contribute my skills and insights to transformative projects that redefine the boundaries of what's achievable in Power, Electromagnetics, and Machine Learning.
